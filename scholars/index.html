The SCRA does not construct its framework from scratch. It draws on fourteen centuries of Islamic metaphysical scholarship — the deepest and most rigorous tradition of ontological inquiry in human history — as well as Western scholars who independently arrived, from within their own tradition, at confirmations of the SCRA's central claims.

The archive also engages systematically with its counter-witnesses: the intellectual formations whose errors are not incidental but structural — whose frameworks actively obstruct the transmission the SCRA documents and must therefore be anatomized with forensic precision.

Primary Custodians

  • Idris / Hermes Trismegistus — Pre-Diluvian originator of the triple science (cosmological, social, material); the chain's first link; his inheritance traced across four millennia in The Custodians of Light
  • Salman al-Farsi — 7th C CE · The Salmanic Precedent: the non-Arab who traversed multiple dispensations to reach the genuine Prophetic reality; architectural template for the SCRA's theory of non-state transmission
  • Al-Kindi — 9th C CE · Baghdad · "Truth is not the property of any single nation or civilization." First systematic articulation of the Open Corridor principle as Islamic philosophical doctrine
  • Ibn Arabi — 11th–12th C CE · Andalusia–Damascus · Shaykh al-Akbar · Futuhat al-Makkiyya and Fusus al-Hikam as the encyclopedic map of the entire transmission architecture; the full conceptual apparatus of the SCRA
  • Suhrawardi — 12th C CE · Aleppo · Hikmat al-Ishraq · Illuminationist philosophy as systematic recovery of the pre-Diluvian light-science; executed at 36 for threatening the established order
  • Mulla Sadra — 17th C CE · Shiraz–Kahak · Asalat al-Wujud · Tashkik al-Wujud · Harakat al-Jawhariyyah · the metaphysical revolution deployed throughout the SCRA; the final synthesis of the Islamic metaphysical tradition
  • Sultan Bahu — 17th C CE · Punjab · Qadiri Sufi Master · "The Qutb serves the Real, not the Sultan." The constitutional principle of the Alid-Sufi formation stated with the precision of a legal maxim
  • Waris Shah · Bullhe Shah — 18th C CE · Punjab · Heer (1766) as a precision instrument of Wahdat al-Wujud metaphysics; kafis as portable philosophical statements accessible across every social stratum
  • Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai — 18th C CE · Sindh · Shah Jo Risalo as the Sindhi counterpart to the Punjabi Sufi canon; Sassi-Punnhu as the phenomenology of exile and the fana of the soul in search of the Real

Western Confirmations

  • Henry Corbin — 20th C CE · Paris · Islamologist and phenomenologist · His work on Ishraq, the Imaginal World, Shi'i gnosis, and the Hidden Imam independently confirmed — from within Western phenomenology — the precise metaphysical claims the SCRA maps
  • Seyyed Hossein Nasr — 20th C CE · Islamic Philosophy · Perennial Tradition · Encyclopedic mapping of the Islamic intellectual tradition as a living, coherent whole; his account of the Safavid synthesis essential to the Khorasan Codes framework
  • F.A. Hayek · Michael Polanyi · Clifford Geertz — Western confirmations of the 'Urf-Fitrah synthesis: decentralized knowledge, tacit knowledge, and the defense of the particular as civilizational infrastructure — deployed in The Open Corridors, Chapter VII
